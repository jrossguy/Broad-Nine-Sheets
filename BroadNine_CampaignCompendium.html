PART 1: THE WORLD

Overview

Earth 9 is the current Earth — the real planet, the real present day — where conventional geopolitics runs alongside supernatural power systems that have no equivalent in recorded history. Nen. The Force. Natural Attunement. Technologies beyond conventional science. None of these existed here until they did, and no one fully understands why this world developed them. The world is approaching something. Those with the sensitivity to perceive it — through Nen, through prophecy, through sheer proximity to power — can feel it building. Factions are positioning. Weapons are being secured. Chosen individuals are being watched. Nobody agrees on what it is.

MAJOR FACTIONS — WESTERN POWERS

🇺🇸 The American Military

General Alignment: Technology, Economic Dominance, Covert Operations Territory: Continental United States and Canada — Canada functions as administered territory, deeply integrated into American defense infrastructure with shared command structures and resource access Friendly toward: Eastern island allies, Northern researchers, United Knights Hostile toward: Southern tribal territories, Amazonian preserve Leadership: President Tronald Dump; cabinet of ten

The wealthiest military force in the world and the most covert. America's power is less about direct military supremacy than about economic control, weapon manufacturing, and the ability to place agents in every region simultaneously. They fight proxy wars, fund instability where it serves them, and maintain diplomatic relationships with enough factions to always have a door open.

The government operates through a cabinet of ten: the President, Vice President, Secretary of Defense, Secretary of State, Secretary of Intelligence, Head of Nen & Special Forces, National Security Advisor, Secretary of Science & Technology, Secretary of the Treasury, and Attorney General. The cabinet is not unified. Political tensions following a recent election scandal have fractured internal trust, and the relationship between the civilian leadership and the Nen division in particular is a source of ongoing friction that has not publicly surfaced.

The Nen & Special Forces division is a fully official military branch whose rank structure sits outside conventional hierarchy. It represents the largest organized Nen user corps in the Western world and is the primary driver behind America's current push to assert dominance in the Southern territories — a campaign that has become a central point of internal political conflict.

Their most powerful field asset operates with an agenda that may not fully align with official American interests.

Power level: Multiple Exalted Weapons in active use and secured storage. Large organized Nen user corps through special operations. One asset of extraordinary individual power operating in a leadership role.

⚔️ The United Knights

General Alignment: Martial Tradition, Expansion, Religious Foundation, Anti-Nen Allied with: Roman-style assassin network, America Leader: King Derek VI Territory: Western Europe and expanding — Scandinavia absorbed, Germany next

The United Knights are a modernized martial empire built on the founding legacy of Carolus Aldric the Ironforged, the first king, who forged the original Fylgja (Bankai) — a soul-bound weapon — in the volcano Aldricseld and used it to unite the fractured kingdoms of Western Europe into a single power. That event is the founding myth, the religious center, and the military philosophy of the Knights simultaneously.

The Sacred Island — Aldricslond The island of Iceland is sacred to Knight culture and is called Aldricslond — the land of Aldric. It is not a tourist destination. Foreign presence on the island is treated as a transgression. At the island's heart is the volcano Aldricseld, a site of deep cultural and supernatural significance. Within the volcano's interior, an ancient primordial entity of extraordinary strength was slain by Carolus Aldric. Two trials guard access to the body: a Trial of Strength and a Trial of Endurance — both are real, both are dangerous, and both can kill. Those who survive both may descend to the body, mix their blood with the creature's, apply it to their chosen weapon, and forge it in the lava. The result is a Fylgja (Bankai) — see the Fylgja (Bankai) section for full mechanics.

Anti-Nen as Law The Knights do not merely distrust Nen — they legislate against it. Countermeasures against Nen users are built into their military training, their equipment, and their tactical doctrine. Nen use within Knight territory is a criminal offense. They view Nen as an impure power compared to the direct soul-expression of the Fylgja (Bankai), and enforce that belief with institutional thoroughness. Confirmed Nen users captured in Knight territory are not released.

Current Leadership and Internal Tensions King Derek VI recently ascended to sole military command following the death of the previous monarch. He is supported by a primary council of generals and advisors and personally leads the elite 200th Battalion. The expansion campaign — already encompassing most of Western Europe and currently pushing through Scandinavia — continues to accelerate.

The population is increasingly tired. A generation of expansion has produced resource strain, manpower losses, and a growing distrust of the royal family among ordinary citizens. The Knights' external strength masks an internal fracture that has not yet found a breaking point. Several figures within Derek's inner council hold influence placed there by outside interests who do not share the Knights' long-term goals.

Deep Storage The Knights' secured vaults include weapons that no current member has been able to wield. Among them is at least one weapon of extraordinary and terrible history. The weapon is inert.

Power level: Several Exalted Weapons in both active use and deep storage. Organized corps of Fylgja (Bankai) wielders. Anti-Nen infrastructure across all controlled territory. At least one weapon in secured storage whose nature has not been publicly confirmed.

🌿 The South American Preserve

General Alignment: Nature, Force tradition, Nen, absolute rejection of technology Location: Northern half of South America — the Amazon and surrounding territory Allied with: Eastern Island Warriors, Religious Territories Hostile toward: American military operations as currently directed; the driving agenda behind the Nen division's Southern campaign specifically Leadership: Marjitani (founder, spiritual authority); Katara and Hal Jordan (operational leadership)

The Preserve is one of the oldest continuous communities on Earth and one of the largest territories held by a single non-governmental faction. It was founded at the convergence of two traditions that arrived together and have coexisted ever since.

One tradition arrived from elsewhere and chose to stay. The Force they brought is not separate from Nen — it is an expression of it, specific to their tradition and training. They planted roots here. Those roots have been growing for a very long time.

Marjitani leads both traditions and has led them for longer than most institutions on Earth have existed. She can no longer move but her presence is felt throughout the territory in ways that defy easy description. Her two children — Katara and Hal Jordan — run the Preserve's day-to-day operations and defense. The three together represent a power tier that has given even the American Nen division reason to plan carefully rather than act quickly.

The Preserve rejects technology without exception. No firearms. No vehicles. No digital systems. What they have in place of technology is the Amazon itself, the Force, Nen at extraordinary depth, and the complete conviction that what they are protecting matters.

The pressure from American military operations is escalating. The Tribes who sheltered at the Preserve's northern border have already been displaced. The Preserve has repelled multiple incursions but the campaign shows no sign of slowing. They know what is driving it. Several of their number know considerably more about the reason than they share publicly.

Power level: Multiple Force users and Nen practitioners of varying tier across a vast territory. Leadership operating at exceptional individual power levels. The Amazon as both home and weapon.

MAJOR FACTIONS — THE WILD CARD

Russia, China, and the African Legion form no formal alliance, but they share a structural position: each is independently powerful, each is led by at least one Level 30 individual, and each is fundamentally opposed to the Western powers. They are the unpredictable variable in every major conflict — willing to cooperate when it serves them, capable of breaking any agreement when it doesn't.

Unlike the Western factions, which operate through institutional inertia, the Wild Card factions are driven by the will of singular, near-mythic individuals. The institution serves the leader, not the reverse.

🐻 The Russian Federation

General Alignment: Military Machinery, Resource Power, Manufacturing Friendly toward: German engineers, Eastern Chinese loyalists Hostile toward: Eastern island warriors, Religious southern territories Leader: Tsar Nikolai Belkin (Immortal)

Russia's strength is industrial and enduring. Their control of the ___ m___l gives them access to the Monster Weapon Forge — a massive remote research facility that harnesses the natural abilities and strengths of slain monsters to produce weapons between Grade 4 and 5 that no conventional manufacturer can replicate. Russia makes these at scale. The Forge is Krisko's domain, and its products have appeared on battlefields across four continents.

Belkin's command operates through a small inner council of division heads. Their names and roles are not publicly confirmed — foreign intelligence services have partial information at best.

Frontline reports from Russian military engagements consistently describe irregular combatants of unusual physical capability alongside conventional forces. The nature and origin of these combatants is officially unacknowledged by Russia. Most foreign governments have assessed the situation privately.

Russia's strategic philosophy is patience. They believe science is the future of strength and have benefited significantly from defectors across the scientific community. They are not interested in winning the next battle. They are interested in being the last power standing.

Power level: One _m_____. Deep military tradition with above-average Nen presence in leadership. Grade 4–5 weapon production at scale.

🐉 The Chinese Imperium

General Alignment: Tradition, Internal Hierarchy, Depth of History Friendly toward: German engineers Hostile toward: Eastern island warriors, Religious southern territories Leadership: The Emperor (Immortal); deep layered hierarchy beneath

China presents itself as a civilization rather than a faction — ancient, self-contained, and operating on timescales that make conventional politics look shortsighted. The Emperor is immortal. The government beneath him is layered enough that even well-placed intelligence assets rarely see more than a few levels deep. What China knows, China keeps.

China is broadly excellent. Their technology is advanced, their Nen tradition is deep and disciplined, and their history has produced weapons that other factions have spent centuries attempting to find. No outside organization — including the two Tier 0 powers — has successfully placed an operative inside China's upper structure. Both monitor China closely. Neither has managed to do more than that.

Their most dangerous active asset operates in the field and does not fully understand what he is. This is not carelessness on China's part.

Exalted Weapons China holds multiple Exalted Weapons. What they are, who holds them, and whether any are currently active is not publicly confirmed.

Power level: One _m_____. Multiple Exalted Weapons including one awaiting a worthy user. Depth across technology, Nen, and military tradition that no single-axis faction can match. One unknown field asset of extraordinary capability.

⚔️ The African Legion

General Alignment: Supernatural Muscle, Aggressive Expansion, Anti-Knight Territory: Upper two-thirds of Africa — north of the DRC and Tanzania boundary Allied with: — Hostile toward: United Knights (primary) Aware of: Both the _ and The __ Leader: Hasim Banales (Most Wanted #3)

The Legion is the most openly aggressive faction on the board. Their leader generates a Nen field so large it is detectable across continental distances and passively clashes with other major Nen fields. Military Depth The Legion has accumulated a significant collection of Force-wielders, Nen users, and supernatural fighters over decades of expansion and targeted recruitment. Their accumulated firepower in individual combatants is the highest of any faction that isn't a Tier 0 power.

Deep Storage The Legion's secured storage contains at least one extraordinary weapon. The conditions for its use are known only to Hasim.

🕌 The Religious Territories

General Alignment: Devotion, supernatural covenant, insular governance Location: Arabian Peninsula, Middle East, and Indian subcontinent Allied with: The Preserve, Eastern Island Warriors, the Knights Hostile toward: Those who violate territorial law or interfere in covenant matters

The Religious Territories span from the Arabian Peninsula through the broader Middle East and across the Indian subcontinent — one of the most densely populated and religiously significant regions on Earth. Different cultures within this vast territory interpret the faith differently. Some focus on angelic covenants, some demonic, some both equally. The governing philosophy is the same across all of them: the divine is real, it can be reached, and the proof has been walked through in person.

Their faith is not theoretical. It is built on direct and repeatable access to a source that no outside scholar has been permitted to study and no intelligence agency has successfully penetrated. What they have encountered, and what returned with them, is the foundation of everything they believe and everything they practice.

The government and its people enter formal pacts with named entities whose nature is connected to that source. A pact grants power as an extension of a relationship — not generosity.

Both sides of the covenant are honored. Neither Heaven nor Hell holds exclusive claim over the territory's faith or its power. Practitioners maintain active relationships with named entities on both sides. This balance — worshipping both equally — is what makes the Territories unique among faiths on Earth. A practitioner in Arabia may focus on angelic covenants. One in the Indian subcontinent may focus on demonic. Both are considered equally faithful.

Deep within the territory stand two figures — monumental in scale, turned to stone mid-conflict centuries ago. They are the territory's most significant monuments and the objects of its deepest worship. They are also slowly changing. The Territories' own scholars have been tracking something for decades. When whatever process is underway completes, the consequences for anything nearby are expected to be catastrophic.

Foreign law applies to all visitors without exception. No external faction has successfully intervened in a territorial legal proceeding. Several have tried.

Power level: Pact-wielders operating at scales that conventional metrics cannot categorize. The moment the petrified beings wake, the territory becomes the most dangerous location on Earth.

MINOR FACTIONS

German Engineering

Location: Central Europe, primarily Germany General Alignment: Scientific advancement, human betterment through technology Allied with: Russia, Chinese loyalists Hostile toward: The Knights (current existential conflict), the Preserve, Religious Territories

Led by a scientific council whose stated mission is the betterment of the human condition through technology and engineering. They are not a government — they are an institution embedded within one, directing research across military, medical, and civilian fronts simultaneously. Their weapons program includes some of the most advanced conventional and chemical technology in the world. Their humanitarian work is equally genuine. The tension between those two aspects of their mandate is ongoing and internal.

Their current strategic position is dire. King Derek VI's expansion has placed German territory directly in the Knights' path. The council has not capitulated, and weapons development continues, but multiple intelligence services assess that Germany falls within the next major conflict cycle unless outside intervention changes the calculus.

Power level: No supernatural presence. They compensate entirely through technology. Their nerve agent program alone makes them dangerous at any scale of engagement. Their value to Russia and China as a weapons supplier is the primary reason outside powers have not allowed them to be absorbed quietly.

Eastern Island Warriors

Location: Japan and surrounding islands General Alignment: Defense, self-sufficiency, preservation of order Allied with: America, the Preserve, Religious Territories (formal); Japanese government (informal mutual understanding) Hostile toward: None publicly — deep institutional resentment toward China

A hidden order operating in parallel with the Japanese government. Known to officials, invisible to the public. Their relationship with Japan is one of long-standing mutual understanding — they defend the islands; Japan provides them the space to exist without interference. They do not recruit broadly and do not advertise their presence.

Internally they represent one of the highest concentrations of trained Nen users outside the major factions. They have also accumulated significant stolen technology over centuries of repelling foreign attempts to access their territory. Every piece of stolen tech in their arsenal was taken from someone who underestimated them. They hate to leave their own shores — every deployment outside Japan is treated as a last resort, and their leadership opposes it almost universally.

China has historically used political and strategic leverage to strong-arm them into cooperation when it served Chinese interests. The Eastern Island Warriors comply when they must and remember every instance. They do not consider China an ally regardless of formal appearances.

Multiple Exalted Weapons remain in their secured storage awaiting candidates. The criteria for worthiness is internal, undocumented externally, and enforced absolutely.

Power level: High Nen concentration. Accumulated stolen technology. Multiple Exalted Weapons in reserve. Entirely unwilling to project power beyond their own territory.

Assassin Network

Location: Rome and satellite cells globally General Alignment: Contract operations, intelligence, Knights support Allied with: The United Knights (formal) Hostile toward: African Legion

Historically operating as independent contractors and intelligence brokers, the Network was structurally destabilized when the African Legion executed a targeted political coup — four of their ten elders were killed in what was framed as separate incidents but is now understood internally as coordinated elimination. The loss triggered a succession crisis and left the organization's direction contested.

In the aftermath, the organization moved toward formal alignment with the Knights — a consolidation that has given them resources their pre-coup operation lacked. The surviving elder seats are still contested. They are rebuilding into something different from what they were.

Power level: Individually exceptional operators. Collectively reduced from peak capacity but actively rebuilding with Knight and covert institutional backing. Their intelligence network remains largely intact — it was the leadership, not the apparatus, that was targeted.

Central American Tribes

Location: Central America — formerly. Refugee population now within the Preserve. General Alignment: Survival Allied with: South American Preserve (refuge) Hostile toward: American military operations

A collection of distinct tribal groups who held Central American territory and practiced basic Nen traditions — sufficient to sustain themselves, not sufficient to defend against organized military pressure. Their security was historically underwritten by the Preserve's protective presence to the south.

That protection failed. American operations pushed into tribal territory with sustained force. The Tribes could not hold. Most of the surviving population now has refuge within the Preserve's borders. Casualties among those who did not reach safety were significant. Captured individuals remain unaccounted for.

Whatever territory the Tribes nominally claim is held in name only. They are not a military force. They are survivors waiting to see what happens to the Preserve that shelters them.

Power level: Minimal. Basic Nen users. Their relevance is now humanitarian and diplomatic rather than strategic.

Arctic Research Consortium

Location: Two permanent installations — North Pole and South Pole General Alignment: Research (humanitarian in the North; military in the South) Allied with: Germany, America Hostile toward: None publicly

Two facilities operating under the same institutional banner with entirely separate mandates and cultures.

North Pole Facility — Altruistic humanitarian research The North facility is staffed by researchers who, by most accounts, genuinely believe in what they are doing. Their active fields include: disease treatment in extreme and remote environments; climate science and ecological preservation; food security technology for resource-scarce regions; advanced prosthetics and mobility restoration; neurological research including early-stage cognitive enhancement; water purification systems for post-disaster deployment; and renewable energy generation in isolated conditions. Their work is published, peer-reviewed, and widely distributed. No military funding is acknowledged.

South Pole Facility — Weapons development — currently rebuilding The South facility is a different institution wearing the same name. Active research areas include: Android design, behavioral programming, and combat application; biological warfare compound development including nerve agent derivatives; directed energy weapons systems; genetic enhancement protocols connected to the Super Soldier research line; cybernetic combat augmentation; Nen suppression and detection technology; and atmospheric weaponization. The facility was attacked approximately two years ago. The attack has not been publicly attributed. Research slowed significantly during reconstruction but did not stop — critical projects were preserved and continued at reduced capacity. Full operational status is expected within the year.

Both facilities maintain formal independence from military command structures. The South's funding history tells a different story.

Power level: No combat capability of their own. Their value is what they produce and what they know. The South facility's android research in particular is among the most advanced outside of Tesla's direct work.

Ark Society

Location: Arabia, Japan, and the South American Preserve General Alignment: Altruistic intervention, universal assistance Allied with: Anyone in need

Led by the Sakura Samurai (Takezo). No political agenda, no territorial claim, no faction loyalty. The Ark Society responds to crises, protects civilians, and assists anyone in need regardless of faction affiliation. They have intervened in active conflict zones without invitation, extracted targets under faction protection, and provided aid to groups that established powers would prefer remain isolated.

Three siblings whose shared Nen ability maintains constant cross-continental communication between their respective postings in Arabia, Japan, and the Preserve. The organization recruits from every faction and every background. Its only apparent criterion is willingness.

Every established power finds them threatening. Not because they are violent — they rarely initiate conflict — but because an armed, mobile organization with Exalted Weapon access, cross-continental reach, and zero political accountability cannot be negotiated with, bought, or redirected. Their intentions being good makes them harder to oppose publicly, not easier to manage operationally.

Power level: Exalted Weapon. Multiple high-level operators. Triplet Nen coordination. The Society's ceiling is unclear and untested.

Vanguard Protocol

Location: Undisclosed facilities in Southeast Asia and Eastern Europe General Alignment: Mercenary neutrality — sell to the highest bidder Allied with: No permanent alliances

An autonomous military technology collective that does not field soldiers. Instead they sell battlefield control — drone swarms, automated defense systems, and AI-directed weapons platforms that can be deployed, managed, and recalled remotely. Their clients do the fighting. Vanguard provides the advantage.

Currently hold active contracts with at least three major factions simultaneously. Each client believes Vanguard is loyal to them. None of them are entirely wrong, and none of them are entirely right. Their neutrality is mercenary rather than principled — any faction with sufficient resources can buy their services, which makes them simultaneously trusted and deeply distrusted by everyone.

No Nen presence whatsoever. Their edge is entirely technological, and it is substantial.

Power level: No individual combat capability. Operationally they can shift the outcome of any conventional military engagement their client is involved in. Their value is leverage, not force.

Prometheus Cartel

Location: East Asia and Eastern Europe General Alignment: Commercial — sell anything to anyone Allied with: No one. Hostile to no one.

An international black market network specializing in cybernetic augmentations, experimental serums, and Grade 3–4 weapons. Their client list includes members of every major and minor faction. They have no ideology, no preferred outcome, and no loyalty beyond the transaction. Their augmentation surgeons are the best outside of Tesla's direct work. Their weapon supply lines are fast, discreet, and consistent.

Loosely connected to Tommy Park's distribution network. The exact nature of that relationship is unclear to outside observers.

Power level: No military presence. Their power is economic and logistical. Anyone who wants something they shouldn't have comes to them eventually.

The Free Seas — Pirates

Territory: International waters — effectively all ocean beyond coastal patrol zones General Alignment: Freedom, individual authority, self-determination Structure: Decentralized. No unified command. Multiple independent crews with alliance clusters between those who share a code.

The ocean is pirate territory. Beyond the coastal safe zones maintained by major factions, any vessel without protection is subject to scrutiny, toll, or boarding at the discretion of whatever crew claims that stretch of water. There are two ways to cross open ocean: pay a recognized government for official passage papers, or negotiate directly with the crew whose territory you are entering. Neither guarantees safety. Both reduce the probability of losing your ship and everything on it. The price scales with perceived value and risk. An empty fishing vessel pays differently than a weapons transport.

Individual crews vary enormously in size, philosophy, and capability. The weakest are opportunistic and disorganized. The strongest can apply military pressure comparable to a minor faction. Some captains have reached Level 30 — genuinely singular individuals whose reputation alone shapes how the sea lanes function across entire regions. A top-tier captain and crew, fully committed, can match the military output of any minor faction. No crew has tested itself against a major faction in a sustained engagement. None of them are looking to.

Alliance clusters exist between crews who share a code — a common belief about freedom, conduct, or purpose. These clusters operate as loose regional powers without formal governance. No alliance has unified enough to become something larger, and most captains prefer it that way.

The philosophy, where it runs deep, is freedom — freedom of movement, freedom from governance, freedom to define your own terms. Most pirates are not criminals in their own understanding. They are free. What the land-based powers call crime, they call living.

On the question of immortality — no pirate worth their reputation would choose it. A short life but a merry one. The most powerful captains on the sea are Level 30 by any measure, sterile as that ceiling demands, and aging toward a death they chose over eternity without hesitation. That choice is not weakness. It is the philosophy made flesh.

Power level: No unified military structure. Individually, top crews rival minor factions. Some captains have reached Level 30 — Apex tier by choice, as no pirate would choose immortality. Collectively, pirate presence controls the majority of the world's navigable ocean — a geographic fact that every navy and merchant operation has to account for. The one organized opposition to pirate dominance of open water is the Marines — a separate naval faction that controls stretches of ocean territory, charges for passage, and maintains an institutional grudge against piracy that does not require provocation to activate.

The Aussies

Location: Australia General Alignment: Independent — loose law, personal authority, coastal commerce Allied with: No formal alliances. Cordial with pirate networks.

Australia operates on a city-by-city basis. Some settlements have their own sheriffs, codes, and courts. Others have nothing at all. The interior is largely self-governed, subject to whoever has the force of will and the backup to enforce their preferences. The denser population centers sit on the coast.

The coastal cities — particularly the major ports — function as pirate sanctuaries. Crews resupply, spend money, seek medical care, and avoid the law without significant risk of interference. Port officials collect taxes on commerce. The right payments ensure official blindness to everything else. It is a system that works because everyone involved benefits from it working. Think Kingston or Havana at the height of their pirate eras: the money flows, the law is selective, and the wrong questions go unanswered.

The Aussie government, such as it is, maintains order in a handful of key cities and collects port revenue. Beyond that, authority is personal. Two individuals of particular note operate within the territory with significant presence and reputation: Android #7 John Arthur — the most accurate shooter in the world, operating as an outlaw with dual pistols — and at least one other figure whose capabilities and profile place them outside any conventional category.

Power level: No standing military. Individual outliers of exceptional capability. Strategically relevant as the Pacific's primary pirate sanctuary and as the last significant unaligned territory in the region.

The Marines

Location: Primary HQ in island Southeast Asia — Singapore, Indonesia, Philippines. Ocean territory extends across open water lanes worldwide. General Alignment: Order, tolled passage, anti-piracy Allied with: No permanent alliances. Pragmatic relationships with major factions when interests align. Hostile toward: Pirates — a standing institutional grudge that does not require provocation to activate

The Marines are the closest thing the open ocean has to a governing authority, and the closest thing the Pirates have to a natural enemy. Their primary headquarters sits in the island chains of Southeast Asia — Singapore, Indonesia, and the Philippines — placing them at the crossroads of the Pacific and Indian Ocean lanes and directly adjacent to Australian pirate sanctuary territory. The proximity to the Aussies is not accidental.

They control stretches of ocean territory and charge any faction or vessel for the right to pass through it cleanly. Major factions pay. Smaller operators negotiate or avoid. Those who do neither discover what the Marines' response capacity looks like.

Their conflict with Pirates is categorical rather than situational — they do not require a specific incident to pursue a pirate crew. The grudge is structural, inherited, and mutual. Against every other faction their posture is transactional: they have clashed with major powers over access disputes and tolling rights without those conflicts escalating to full war. They are not looking for a land war. They are looking at the sea.

Several Marine commanders have reached or are approaching Level 30. As a naval force they represent the single largest organized obstacle to uncontested pirate dominance of open water.

Power level: Multiple Level 30 commanders. Significant naval force. Strategic HQ position at the Pacific-Indian Ocean crossroads. The only faction whose entire strategic purpose is defined by opposition to another.

The Cape Consortium

The Bounty System

Bounties in Earth 9 are not issued by a single authority — they are placed by factions, criminal networks, intelligence agencies, and powerful individuals operating independently. A bounty represents a consensus of hostility: enough parties agree that someone is dangerous, valuable, or both to put resources behind finding them. The Most Wanted list is the clearest expression of that consensus.

How Bounties Work

Any faction with sufficient resources can place a bounty. The amount reflects both the difficulty of the target and the urgency of the issuing party. Bounties stack — if three factions independently post on the same individual, the listed amount is the sum. Multiple bounties from multiple sources are treated as one consolidated figure for the purposes of enforcement.

Bounties can be collected dead or alive unless the posting faction specifies otherwise. Factions paying for live capture are almost always after information, not justice.

The black market functions as the primary bounty clearinghouse. Information brokers track known bounties and can provide current figures for a fee.

On Level 30 Individuals

Reaching level 30 is not merely a personal achievement. It represents a category of existence that almost no one in the world occupies. These individuals are not powerful in the way a well-trained fighter is powerful. They are powerful in the way that geography is powerful.

A universal consequence of Level 30: Every being who reaches this threshold — without exception — becomes sterile. The body redirects everything into sustaining and expressing that power. It stops producing. This is not a side effect of immortality specifically. It is a condition of the ceiling itself.

Two kinds of Level 30 exist:

True Immortals cannot die of natural causes. They do not age past the point they reached 30. They have in most cases lived for centuries. The word carries its full biological weight.

Apex characters are also Level 30 — equally powerful, equally sterile — but they age normally and can die naturally. Most had the opportunity to pursue true immortality and chose not to. That choice is personal, specific, and defines something essential about who they are. Some find mortality preferable. Some find the idea of living forever horrifying. Some simply wanted to die on their own terms.

Level 30 individuals exist within most major factions — some hold more than others. Their distribution is one of the most closely tracked and least publicly confirmed pieces of intelligence in the world. Most of the world's named threats are Legendary-tier at best. Encountering a genuine Level 30 individual is an event, not an encounter.

The ceiling: Level 30 is understood to be the absolute limit of what any living being can achieve. Every framework of power — Nen, Technology, physical training, supernatural origin — converges here and goes no further.

A note on temperament: Most Level 30 individuals are profoundly selfish about their time and attention. They will answer the call of their faction when the situation genuinely demands it — but they resist being summoned for matters beneath them, and their threshold for "beneath them" is very high. They are powerful enough that no one can force them to care. Some are deeply dedicated to their cause. Most are not those individuals. Most will arrive when they feel like it, do what they judge necessary, and leave without explaining themselves to anyone.

The Fylgja (Bankai) System

A note on soul investment: The Fylgja and Nen both use the soul as their foundational development tool. They are fundamentally opposed — the soul cannot simultaneously be the forge (Fylgja) and the furnace (Nen). No practitioner in history has held both at full development simultaneously.

Associated with the United Knights. The process and full depth of effects are not publicly known outside the order.

In Norse mythology, a Fylgja (Bankai) is the spiritual double of a person — a manifestation of their soul that follows them through life. The Knights took that name for their most sacred weapon tradition. A Fylgja (Bankai) is not a weapon you find or buy. It is a weapon you become.

Initiative

At the start of combat, every participant rolls initiative to determine turn order. Highest result goes first; ties broken by Dexterity score.

Initiative Roll = [Tier dice] + Dexterity modifier

Dexterity is the sole driver of initiative — physical reaction speed determines who acts first. No other attribute contributes passively. Dexterity Training Tier 11 overrides the roll entirely: that character always acts first regardless of what anyone else rolls. Tiebreaks between two Dex Tier 11 characters go to the higher Dexterity score.

Surprise: If a character is unaware of combat starting (ambushed, distracted, sleeping), they do not roll initiative for the first round — they act last in round 1, then roll normally from round 2 onward. Assassination from Stealth uses separate rules (see Section 7) and does not use the initiative roll.

Miscellaneous Movement Actions: Small physical interactions during a turn cost movement from the character's pool rather than a separate action type. This keeps the action economy clean — grabbing something on the run or hitting a switch mid-dash doesn't freeze you, it just costs momentum.

Cross-Tier Block and Dodge

When attacker and defender are in different dice tiers, apply the following rule: - If the attacker wins the exchange: use the attacker's tier column for offense bonus damage. - If the defender wins the exchange: use the defender's tier column for damage reduction.

The higher-tier fighter benefits on both sides — their tier brackets produce better outcomes whether attacking or defending. A two-tier gap (Standard vs Legendary, or Elite vs Immortal) creates a genuine uphill battle for the lower-tier combatant: they can still land meaningful hits on good rolls, but their margin cap is lower and their defense brackets protect less.

When the gap is three tiers (Standard vs Immortal), opposed rolls often become impractical for the Standard-tier character. In these cases, treat the lower-tier attacker as rolling against a flat DC (Immortal Moderate = 36) rather than an opposed roll. Beating the DC deals base weapon damage only — no margin bonus — since the Immortal is not meaningfully threatened enough to commit to active defense.

0. Dice Scaling System

The Problem: At high levels modifiers grow large enough that the d20 becomes nearly irrelevant. A modifier of +20 means rolling a 1 still gives 21 — the die only swings results across a narrow band. Rolls stop feeling decisive.

Exalted Weapon Attunement (Level 19): A Grade 5 weapon is not simply wielded — it is earned. Attunement represents a character and weapon reaching a mutual understanding built through shared history.

Pre-Attunement (any level): A character may wield an Exalted Weapon before attunement. However the weapon resists. While wielding it unattuned: −3 to all attack and defense rolls, the weapon's legendary afflictions do not trigger, and the weapon deals its base damage dice only with no additional bonuses. The power is real but the weapon is fighting the wielder as much as the enemy.

Attunement Requirements: The character must have wielded the weapon in at least 3 separate combats before attempting. At Level 19, the character may attempt formal attunement — the specific process is DM-designed and tied to the weapon's history and the character's arc. It should feel earned, not mechanical.

Post-Attunement: All pre-attunement penalties lift. Legendary afflictions activate. The weapon's full damage potential unlocks. In some cases the weapon reveals abilities not visible to previous wielders. Attunement is permanent — the weapon recognizes the character regardless of time apart.

Nen Reactions

Nen does not proc on its own — it reacts. Specific elements, when already active on a target as a proc status, will react when Nen damage then lands. The order is always Element first, Nen second. The target must already be carrying the element's status before the Nen hit arrives.

Only five elements produce Nen reactions. These are the elements whose energies most fundamentally interfere with or are amplified by living Nen.

Shadow → Nen: Extended Blackout A target under Nen Blackout (Shadow proc) who takes Nen damage experiences a deep disruption. The Blackout extends to 2 full turns, and any passive Nen effects the target had active (auras, persistent abilities) are forcibly suppressed for the same duration.

Radiation → Nen: Corrupted Nen A Radiating target hit by Nen has their internal energy contaminated by the radiation. On their next turn, any Nen ability they attempt requires a Nen check (DC = current Radiation tier × 5). Failure: the ability misfires randomly (DM determines direction and effect), action wasted. The Radiation stack is not cleared.

Light → Nen: Purifying Burn A Blinded target (Light proc) hit by Nen amplifies the disruption. The Blindness extends one additional turn, and if the target attempts any Nen ability before it clears, they take D4 Light damage (armor-bypassing) from the rejected energy interaction.

Electric → Nen: Disruption Surge A Stunned target (Electric proc) hit by Nen has the disruption deepened. The Stun extends until the end of the following turn (rather than the current). Any Nen ability attempted before the disruption clears fails automatically with no stamina refund.

Poison → Nen: Toxic Surge A Poisoned target hit by Nen finds the poison amplified by the life-energy flowing into them. The Poison duration extends by 1 turn, and any healing received while both conditions are active is halved.

Design note: Nen reacts to the world; it does not lead the charge. These five reactions represent the elements whose nature most conflicts with living energy — darkness that suppresses it, radiation that corrupts it, light that burns through it, electricity that disrupts its flow, and poison that turns its own healing properties against it.

Elemental Interactions

Interactions trigger when a target affected by one damage type is then hit by a compatible second type. The first type must already be active before the second lands. Unless stated, the original status ends when the interaction resolves.

🔥 Fire

Fire + Water — Extinguished Steam: Water extinguishes fire. Rapid temperature change releases scalding steam in a 1-sq radius (D4 Fire to all in range, including allies). Target is now Soaked.

Fire + Air — Wildfire Burst: Air feeds fire, not extinguishes it. Fire spreads 1-sq radius before Air damage resolves — each character in range makes Dodge (10+) or catches fire. Target then knocked prone from the air force.

Fire + Ice — Flash Freeze: Extreme temperature collision cancels both conditions. No Freeze proc, no Fire continuation. Thermal shock deals +D6 Physical to the target from body/material stress.

Fire + Poison — Toxic Smoke: Burning poison creates 2-sq toxic cloud. Poison on target ends (burned). Any character in the cloud next turn makes Constitution (12+) or becomes Poisoned 1 turn. Cloud persists until attacker's next turn start.

Fire + Earth — Scorched Ground: Heat bakes the surrounding earth. Target's square and all adjacent squares become Difficult Terrain for the combat. Targets Rooted in this area have their Root extended 1 turn.

Fire + Shadow — Soulfire: Shadow corrupts rather than extinguishes. Flames turn dark and unnatural, dealing half Fire / half Shadow each tick. Shadow proc (Nen Blackout) applies in addition to the ongoing fire.

💧 Water

Water + Electric — Conductance: If Soaked, Electric damage has base dice doubled before resistances. Soaked ends.

Water + Ice — Flash Freeze Puddle: Ice damage resolves normally. The target's square becomes an Ice Patch — anyone moving through it makes Acrobatics (10+) or falls prone. Patch lasts until attacker's next turn start.

Water + Air — Blinding Mist: 2-sq radius mist cloud. All inside: Awareness −3, ranged attacks −2 to hit through the mist. Disperses end of next round.

Water + Poison — Contaminated Water: Poison proc chance doubles (8 or lower instead of 4). If already Poisoned, duration extends 1 turn.

❄️ Ice

Ice + Earth — Immobilized: Rooted + Frozen = Immobilized for combined duration. No movement, no movement-related actions.

Ice + Electric — Shatter Shock: Electric cracks the ice violently. Frozen ends immediately. Ice shards explode in 1-sq radius for D6 Physical to all in range. Electric damage and its proc apply normally to the original target.

Ice + Sonic — Resonance Shatter: Sonic shatters Frozen ice. Every character within 2 squares takes D4 Physical (no Defense roll) from shards. Original Sonic damage cannot be mitigated as normal.

Ice + Air — Arctic Gust: Movement −2 additional (stacked on Ice penalty). All characters within 2 squares of target lose 1 movement. Frozen proc, if triggered, lasts 1 extra turn.

Electric + Acid — Corrosive Arc: If armor is Corroded and target takes Electric, +D6 Electric through the corroded metal. Corroded armor loses an additional −1 from the electrical stress.

General Interaction Rules: - Resistance applies to interaction bonus damage of that type. - Immunity (Con tier 10, double-stacked) applies to all interaction effects involving that type as trigger or result. - Proc interactions require the proc to be active before the second hit. Simultaneous hits don't self-interact. - Area interactions affect allies. Friendly fire is real.

Proficiency System:

Proficiency is earned through attribute training, not through level-ups. Each physical attribute has a proficiency path:

  • Strength → Melee Weapon Proficiency: Unlocked at Str Training Tier 2. Choose one melee weapon type. Bonus scales with further Str training.
  • Dexterity → Ranged Weapon Proficiency: Unlocked at Dex Training Tier 2. Choose one ranged weapon type. Bonus scales with further Dex training.
  • Constitution: Training Tier 1 grants +2 to any Block and +2 Fortitude.

Before the relevant training tier is reached, characters have no proficiency bonus — they roll base dice and weapon damage only.

Mental Attribute Modifier to Attack Rolls — Choose One:

Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma can each eventually contribute a modifier to attack rolls through their training paths. However, only one of these three may apply to attack rolls at a time. When you first gain one of these bonuses, you declare which attribute you are drawing from. Switching requires a full rest and a conscious decision.

  • Intelligence (Tech): floor(Int÷2) at Tier 5 → full at Tier 9
  • Intelligence (Tactical Analysis): floor(Int÷2) at Tier 4 → full at Tier 9
  • Wisdom (Nen): floor(Wis÷2) at Tier 8 → full at Tier 9 (Nen attacks only)
  • Wisdom (Natural Attunement): floor(Wis÷2) at Tier 6 → full at Tier 9 (Attunement attacks only)
  • Charisma (both paths): floor(Chr÷2) at Tier 4 → full at Tier 9

If you qualify for bonuses from more than one, choose which applies. The others are inactive for attack rolls until you switch. Str modifier to melee attack rolls and Dex modifier to ranged attack rolls are unaffected — this rule applies only to the Int/Wis/Chr modifier bonuses.

Tech Specializations (Int Training Tier 4)

At tier 4, the character establishes their Tech Specialization — the signature system defining their combat identity. It has a Passive, an Active, and a Signature. Unlike a Nen Ability, it can be damaged, upgraded, and modified between sessions.

The four Specializations below define the framework and design philosophy for each type. The actual Passive, Active, and Signature abilities are specific to the character and designed collaboratively with the DM. What follows are guidelines, not predetermined moves.

⚔️ Combat Systems Offensive tech: targeting, weapon modification, precision at any range.

Built around a Targeting System of some kind — HUD, weapon modification, ballistic computer, or other precision framework. This character becomes a precision machine, with consistency of output that Nen users cannot replicate through internal power alone.

Design Guidelines: - Passive: Should reward hitting cleanly — a bonus that triggers when the attack significantly beats the defense roll, representing the targeting system operating as designed - Active: Should extend or concentrate offensive capability — marking a target, overloading a shot, calibrating for a specific threat - Signature: Should feel like total commitment — one moment of absolute precision that resets the battlefield

Enhanced tier (tier 8): The passive condition threshold should lower, making the reward easier to earn in combat. Tier 9: Intelligence modifier applies to all attack rolls.

🛡️ Defensive Systems Countermeasures, reactive armor, turning enemy force against itself.

Built around an Adaptive Shield Array or equivalent — reactive plates, energy absorbers, countermeasure emitters, or programmable barrier tech. This character does not merely absorb damage; they respond to it.

Design Guidelines: - Passive: Should activate at a critical moment — when damage would do something decisive (reduce armor to zero, break through to HP) — and mitigate it - Active: Should create space and protect allies as well as self — repositioning, denial, defensive burst - Signature: Should feel like a complete shutdown — one moment of absolute countermeasure that negates an attack or neutralizes a weapon for the rest of the combat

Enhanced tier (tier 8): The passive should activate more frequently per combat. Tier 9: Intelligence modifier applies to all Defensive Systems defense rolls.

🕵️ Infiltration Systems Stealth tech, electronic warfare, deception, information dominance.

Built around a Personal ECM Suite — signal jammers, holographic projectors, hacking tools, and countermeasures embedded in clothing or equipment. This character rewards patience, preparation, and knowing the field better than anyone else present.

Design Guidelines: - Passive: Should expand stealth capability — substituting Technology for Stealth in proximity to electronics, extending the refresh window, masking sound and movement signatures - Active: Should give access to the enemy's infrastructure — jaming a system, opening a lock, cutting comms, redirecting a camera - Signature: Should make the character genuinely invisible to systems for a short window — untargetable by anything that relies on detection technology

Enhanced tier (tier 8): Active expands to biological targets — the character can mask their presence from passive Nen detection and Nen-based sense abilities, and gains a bonus to Awareness checks when operating against Nen users in the area. Tier 9: Intelligence modifier applies to all Technology checks.

🤖 Autonomous Systems Drones, constructs, robotic companions — extending presence across the field.

Built around a Deployed Unit — a robotic companion, drone swarm, or autonomous construct. This character puts a second body on the field that the enemy must account for. The Unit has its own HP and can be destroyed; it must be rebuilt (Engineering check, 1 session downtime) before redeployment.

The Deployed Unit (baseline stats): - HP: Int modifier × 5 - Movement: equal to character's base movement - Attack: damage die scales with character's dice tier (Standard D6 / Elite D8 / Legendary D10 / Immortal D12); uses character's Technology to hit - One Main Action per round on character's initiative (commanded as Free Action)

Enhanced tier (tier 8): Unit HP should double; it should gain a split-action option. Tier 9: Intelligence modifier applies directly to the Unit's attack rolls.

4d. Natural Attunement System (Wisdom Training Tier 4)

Natural Attunement is the practice of connecting to and directing the natural forces of the world. Unlike Nen — which is internal and inseparable from the self — or Technology — which is built and carried — Natural Attunement draws from the world around you. You are not the source. You are the conductor.

No single culture owns Natural Attunement. Every civilization that has developed it has interpreted it through its own lens. The Preserve focuses on the living world and its plants. Russian tradition emphasizes the animalistic. Elemental traditions are distributed across every continent with no single home. The compendium uses the neutral academic term. Practitioners use whatever their culture calls it.

Established at Wis Training Tier 4 — mutually exclusive with Nen Ability. Choose one permanently.

Connection Pool

The resource system for Natural Attunement. Base: Wis modifier × 10. Grows at tier 5 (+20). Between sessions: always recharges fully regardless of environment. Partial recharge between combats: roll Nature (DC scales with your tier: Standard 12 / Elite 16 / Legendary 22 / Immortal 28) — success restores 10 Connection. This partial recharge requires a natural environment; no combat recharge in sterile or artificial spaces.

The Form

At Tier 4 you establish your Form — the specific, personal expression of your Attunement. No two practitioners share the same Form even within the same path. Developed collaboratively with the DM. At establishment, the Form should be roughly equivalent in power to the character's other Tier 4 abilities — meaningful and personal, but not yet at the level of a fully developed combat system. It grows with the wielder.

Every Form has three components:

Component Cost Description
Passive Free Always active in combat. The ambient expression of your path — plants subtly growing, your companion attentive, your element present in the air around you
Active 10–20 Connection The primary combat expression — used as Main Action or Bonus Action depending on scale
Signature 30–50 Connection Once per combat. The defining move. What people remember.

Cost guidelines: An Active costing 10 Connection fires roughly 5–7 times before the pool is exhausted. One costing 20 fires 3–4 times. Signatures should feel like a meaningful spend.

Weapons and Attunement Style

Natural Attunement can be expressed through any weapon, with a specific preferred weapon as your personal anchor, or with no weapon at all — hands, breath, direct presence. This is a style choice, not a gating mechanic.

With a preferred weapon: Using a specific weapon you have trained alongside your Attunement gives +1 to Attunement-based attack rolls with that weapon. This can be any weapon — a wooden staff, a stone blade, a hunting knife, bare hands wrapped in bark. It is a comfort and proficiency, not a magical bond. Losing it is losing a preferred tool, not a crisis.

Without a specific weapon: No attack roll modifier. Better manipulation range and utility. Preferred by practitioners who favour terrain control, environmental scale effects, or wide-area expressions.

For Elemental practitioners especially: weapons are entirely optional and stylistic. A fire bender might use a flaming blade. Another might never touch a weapon. Both are equally valid. The element is always accessible regardless of what is or isn't in your hands.

Environmental Dependency

Natural Attunement draws from the world around you. In completely sterile, lifeless, or artificial environments — sealed facilities, dead zones, vacuum — your power is diminished or inaccessible depending on your path. This is the built-in balance that Nen and Technology do not share. A Tech user in an empty concrete room still has their gadgets. A Flora practitioner has nothing to work with.

At Wis Training Tier 7 the Form's Active ability can now be used as a Bonus Action instead of a Main Action once per turn — without reducing its power. This frees the Main Action on turns where the Form is the priority, and represents the practitioner's deepening fluency with their path.

4e. The Pact System

Charisma Training Tier 4 — mutually exclusive with the Presence System.

A Pact is a formal covenant between a practitioner and a named supernatural entity. Access requires a specific narrative circumstance — Religious Territories initiation, direct entity contact, an inherited pact, or an entity reaching out. You cannot purchase or train into one without the relationship first.

All entities capable of granting pacts are Level 30 and operate at the highest tier of their respective domain — the upper echelons of Heaven or Hell. Lesser beings cannot grant pacts. The power of the channel reflects not the entity's strength (they are all extraordinary) but what they choose to give and at what cost.

The Pact System is built around a single core concept: Covenant Favor. Outside the channel you have nothing. Inside it you have everything your deity grants. The arc of every fight is building toward the window and deciding when to open it.

ALIGNMENT

Chosen at Chr Training Tier 4. Permanent unless a major narrative event changes it.

  • Angelic — covenant with a being of divine origin
  • Demonic — covenant with a being of infernal origin
  • Balanced — covenants with both simultaneously. Extraordinarily rare. Requires a pre-existing contract with one side before the other can be approached. Story-gated and DM-discretion only. Do not assume this is available.

5. Nen Ability Framework (Wisdom Training Tier 4)

Reaching Wisdom Training tier 4 is the most characterful mechanical moment in the system. The player establishes a Nen Ability — a personal, unique expression of their internal energy that no one else has. It is developed collaboratively with the DM and should feel like it emerges from the character's history, not their stat spread.

Every Nen Ability has three layers. The specific abilities within each layer are character-specific and DM-designed — what follows are guidelines for what each layer should accomplish, not predetermined moves.

Structure: 1. Passive — Always active, free. The ambient expression of the character's Nen. What enemies sense. What observers notice. 2. Active — Costs stamina. The deliberate expression in combat. Usable as long as stamina allows. 3. Signature — High cost, once per combat. The move that defines how this character is remembered.

Stamina Pool: Wis modifier × 10 baseline. Tier 8 adds 30. Emeralds can further expand. Recharges fully between sessions — equivalent to a long rest. No partial recharge between combats. Nen is internal and cannot be refilled mid-session. Stamina costs are designed to be less demanding and less frequent than other resource systems — a pool of 70 should last a full session of normal play without careful conservation.

Stamina Cost Guidelines: When designing a Nen Ability collaboratively, use these ranges as an anchor. Exact costs should be tuned with the DM based on the ability's impact.

Layer Typical Cost Notes
Passive Free Always active; no stamina drain
Active 10–20 stamina Per use; scale up for wider effect or greater damage
Signature 30–50 stamina Once per combat (once per encounter); resets when a new encounter begins. Fleeing and immediately re-engaging does not count as a new combat — the encounter has not ended.

An Active that costs 10 stamina on a pool of 70 fires roughly 7 times before exhaustion — aggressive but sustainable. One that costs 25 fires 2–3 times. Signatures should feel significant to spend.

The Six Categories

Each category defines the fundamental nature of a Nen Ability. The character chooses one category, and their Passive/Active/Signature must fit within its nature — but the specific expression is theirs alone.

Category selection is permanent. A character's Nen category is an expression of who they are — it does not change through training. The only exception is a major narrative shift of significant story weight, requiring DM approval. This is rare and should carry real consequence in the fiction.

💛 Enhancement The most direct path. Raw Nen poured into the body or a held object to amplify its natural limits.

Enhancement users hit harder, endure more, and recover faster. Their power has no conditions, no setup, and no luck required — it is always there. The tradeoff is that it cannot be redirected. Enhancement answers every problem with more force.

Passive guideline: Should reflect the constant presence of Nen in the body — a boost to physical damage, resilience, or recovery speed that operates at all times without activation.

Active guideline: Should involve a concentrated burst of Enhancement — pushing physical output past its sustainable ceiling for a short window (increased damage, increased durability, accelerated healing).

Signature guideline: Should feel like complete, unconstrained output — the character releasing every limiter for 2-3 turns at significant personal cost, or a single strike of absolute physical maximum.

Natural reaction affinity: When elements react with Enhancement-type Nen, Earth and Physical tend to express most naturally — the weight and force of the body.

🔵 Emission Nen detached from the body and projected as an independent force.

Emission users fight at any range with equal effectiveness. Their power is consistent and precise but weaker than Enhancement at close range — detached Nen is always slightly less potent than internal Nen. The tradeoff is that range is never a limiting factor.

Passive guideline: Should extend the effective range of Nen-based actions and create a persistent hazard zone in spaces the character has projected Nen through.

Active guideline: Should involve a focused projection — a ranged Nen attack that can be charged for increased potency and special properties.

Signature guideline: Should involve multiple simultaneous projections — the character filling the space around them with Nen that acts independently, requiring enemies to address multiple sources at once.

Natural reaction affinity: Fire, Light, Electric — elements that project outward.

🟣 Transmutation Nen whose properties are altered to mimic another substance.

Transmutation is the most versatile offensive type. The character can make their Nen behave like anything — elasticity, razors, electricity, adhesives. The aura flickers and shifts with the current property being mimicked. Transmutation's strength is creativity: in the hands of someone who thinks fast, it counters anything.

Passive guideline: Should reflect the inherent instability and adaptability of Transmuted Nen — either free access to any element when using Nen reactions (since the Nen already mimics the element), or a constant low-level property that always runs.

Active guideline: Should allow the character to coat themselves or a weapon in Transmuted Nen with a chosen property, carrying that element's on-hit effect for a short duration.

Signature guideline: Should lock the character's entire output into one chosen element for an extended duration — every physical contact carrying that element's effect.

Natural reaction affinity: Any. Transmutation users face the fewest restrictions when triggering elemental reactions.

🟤 Conjuration Physical objects materialized from Nen. Real, structural, and persistent.

Conjuration creates things that exist in the world — can be touched, damaged, and used. A conjured sword can be handed to an ally. A conjured wall takes real damage. A conjured chain has real tensile strength. The character's power makes the battlefield more complex for everyone in it.

Passive guideline: Should establish the structural integrity of conjured objects — HP scaling, resistance to dispelling, persistence without active maintenance.

Active guideline: Should create one targeted object — a weapon, a barrier, a binding — that serves a clear tactical purpose.

Signature guideline: Should create a large-scale Conjuration that acts as a semi-autonomous presence — something that changes the shape of the entire encounter.

Natural reaction affinity: Earth, Ice, Physical — structure, mass, crystalline solidity.

🔴 Manipulation Control of external objects or beings through Nen.

Manipulation users redirect, reposition, and control. They tend to look calm in combat because the chaos around them is their design. Their power scales with the intelligence of how it's applied — a Manipulation user in a complex environment is extremely dangerous; in an empty room they are more limited.

Passive guideline: Should allow one automatic positional redirection per turn — Nen threads that reposition an incoming threat, pull something out of reach, or intercept a projectile. This is a positional or environmental effect, not a free Block or Dodge roll — it does not reduce damage or replace the defense roll mechanic.

Active guideline: Should attach threads to a target and allow the character to control their positioning — pulling toward or holding in place — for as long as stamina is maintained.

Signature guideline: Should attempt complete control of one target for a short duration — if it succeeds, the character dictates the target's actions entirely.

Natural reaction affinity: Air, Water, Sonic — redirection, flow, positional manipulation.

⚫ Specialization A completely unique ability outside all categories. No two Specialists are alike.

Specialization does not fit the framework above. It does something with no precedent. It is always tied to who the character fundamentally is — their obsession, loss, or defining trait expressed as a supernatural force. No template exists because no template could cover what a Specialist does.

Design requirements: - Must be developed entirely before level-up — not at the table - Must be rooted in the character's backstory, not their combat preferences - Must have an inherent limitation as interesting as the power itself - Must do one thing no other type can do

Cost structure: Specialization follows the same stamina economy as other categories — Passive free, Active 10–20, Signature 30–50. The uniqueness is in the effect, not the economy.

DM guidance: Specialization is the most demanding ability to design and the most memorable to encounter. Budget time for it.

Nen Traditions

Nen goes by different names in different cultures and has been independently discovered in different forms across the world. These are all expressions of the same internal energy — the mechanics are identical, only the flavor and tradition differ.

Tradition Cultural Home Notes
Nen General — no single origin The most widely used term. Standard framework.
The Force South American Preserve A specific order's tradition. Techniques resolve as Nen checks. Force practitioners use Wisdom and follow the same Nen Ability structure — the difference is in lineage, training method, and naming. A character does not need Preserve ties to pursue it, but contact with a practitioner is the normal entry point.

Other traditions may exist. The above are the most widely encountered.

The Pinnacle (Wis Training Tier 11)

At Tier 11, the Nen Ability reaches its Pinnacle — a fourth expression transcending the Signature in scope. Once per session, consuming the Signature activates a state beyond even the Pinnacle's normal scope.

Design guideline: Should represent the Nen Ability at its theoretical maximum — what the ability becomes if every limiter is removed. Cannot be anticipated or defended against by conventional means. Developed entirely before play, never at the table.

7. Stealth

Movement While Sneaking: Moving in stealth costs double movement. A character with 6 base movement effectively has 3 usable squares per turn while sneaking. Detection Ranges — Dynamic by Awareness: Detection range scales 1:1 with the detector's Awareness modifier rather than fixed distances. This keeps stealth tense against high-Awareness enemies without making it trivial against low ones.

Characters with no Nen cannot be detected this way.

9. Skills Reference — Complete Usage Guide

Every skill is listed below with its governing attribute, its baseline use, and all expanded mechanics available in play. Skills marked with a magazine counter on your character sheet can accumulate magazines — consumables found in the world that auto-consume on a relevant roll to grant a flat +5 bonus to that check.

Acrobatics (DEX)

Precision movement, balance, and coordination. The physical counterpart to Athletics — where Athletics is about power, Acrobatics is about control.

  • Landing: Any uncontrolled fall, aerial dismount, or descent from height requires an Acrobatics check to land cleanly. Failure = prone. Critical failure = prone + damage.
  • Terrain Navigation: Moving through difficult terrain (ice, rubble, narrow ledges, moving vehicles) may require Acrobatics to avoid falling or being slowed.
  • Dodge Assist: When a terrain or environmental effect would knock you prone after a successful Dodge, roll Acrobatics (DM-set DC). Success = you maintain footing.
  • Stunt Movement: Vaulting a barrier, sliding under a swing, or passing through an obstacle in one action uses Acrobatics vs the obstacle's DC. Success = pass through without spending extra movement.

Athletics (STR)

The premier physical skill. Covers all power-based physical challenges — every active character encounters these regularly regardless of build.

  • Sprint: Roll Athletics as a Bonus Action to gain bonus movement equal to the Sprint table result. See Section 6.
  • Climb / Swim: Required check each turn in difficult physical terrain. Failure halts movement; critical failure means falling or sinking.
  • Break Free: When restrained, grappled, or bound, contest Athletics vs the restraint's DC or the enemy's Athletics. Success = escape.
  • Feats of Strength: Any narrative physical challenge — forcing a jammed door, lifting debris off an ally, holding a structure in place. High rolls execute quickly or impressively.
  • Resist Knockdown: When a push or knockdown effect targets you, roll Athletics (DC = attacker's STR modifier + 12) to resist. Substitutes for CON when braced strength is the relevant factor.

Awareness (INT)

Perception, detection, and environmental reading. The primary skill used to counter Stealth, spot ambushes, and notice details others miss.

  • Passive Detection: Always active. Detection radius = 20ft + Awareness modifier (ft). Combat-ready environments add +5. See Section 7 for full detection rules.
  • Active Search: Declare you are actively scanning — detection radius doubles to 40ft + Awareness modifier. Requires your action or Bonus Action.
  • Spot Hidden: Contested vs a target's Stealth. Beating by 1–4 senses a presence with no location; beating by 5+ gives full location.
  • Ambush Defense: A successful Awareness check before combat starts negates Surprise — you act normally in round 1 rather than acting last.
  • Tactical Analysis: At INT Training Tier 4, once per combat as a Bonus Action make a contested Awareness check against one enemy. On success, gain +4 to your next defense or attack roll against them.
  • Read a Magazine: Unknown magazines can be identified with an Awareness or Linguistics check, or during downtime. Once identified you recognize that type on sight.

Engineering (INT)

Mechanical construction, structural analysis, and field repair of complex systems. Paired with Technology for the full INT skill suite.

  • Repair Kit Use: Roll Engineering to restore Armor HP. Results below 5 restore nothing — minimum competence required. See Section 2 for the full roll table.
  • Gadget Crafting: Unlocked at INT Training Tier 4. Costs Supplies and an Engineering check. See Section 4b.
  • Structural Assessment: Roll Engineering to evaluate a structure for load capacity, weaknesses, or sabotage points. DC 12 = general assessment; DC 20 = precise weak points.
  • Field Improvisation: Constructing a makeshift tool, trap, or barricade from available materials. DM-set DC based on complexity.
  • Vehicle Repair: Damaged vehicles repaired between sessions or during downtime with Engineering checks. DC scales with vehicle class and damage severity.
  • Auto-Success: At INT Training Tier 4+, once per session declare an automatic success on any Engineering or Technology check before rolling.

Fortitude (CON)

Physical and supernatural resilience. Governs the body's ability to resist damage, push through punishment, and endure effects that would break lesser characters.

  • Resist Knockdown: Default CON check when a push or knockdown effect targets you (DC = attacker's STR modifier + 12).
  • Endurance: Sustained physical effort under pressure — holding a position, staying conscious after massive damage — uses Fortitude.
  • Poison / Disease Resistance: When afflicted, Fortitude checks determine duration and severity. Higher results reduce tick damage or clear the effect faster.
  • Supernatural Status Resistance: At CON Training Tier 5, Fortitude covers resistance to Nen Disruption, Cursed, and Corrupted effects in addition to physical conditions.
  • CON Tier 1 Bonus: +2 to any Block and +2 Fortitude granted passively at Training Tier 1.

Heal (WIS)

Battlefield medicine, triage, and recovery. Governs the use of Healing Charges and treatment of ongoing conditions.

  • Healing Charge Use: Roll Heal to restore HP. Result determines HP restored. See Section 2 for the full roll table.
  • Treat Poison: Roll Heal (DC 21+) to remove a Poison status from yourself or an adjacent ally.
  • Treat Radiation: Roll Heal (DC = current radiation tier × 3) to reduce radiation stack by one tier per success.
  • Stabilize: Roll Heal to stabilize a Downed ally. Three successes before three failures brings them back to 1 HP.
  • Field Diagnosis: Heal check (DC 12) before treating a condition identifies it correctly. Treating without diagnosis adds +4 to all subsequent Heal DCs this session.
  • Armor Restore (Jade Emerald): The Jade Nen Emerald grants Healing Charges the ability to also restore Armor HP equal to half the rolled value.

Intimidate (CHR)

Projecting threat, forcing compliance through fear, and breaking composure. Both a social and a combat skill.

  • Social Intimidation: Opposed Intimidate vs target's Sense Motive. On a strong margin (beat by 10+) target volunteers additional information or concessions.
  • Combat Intimidate: Unlocked at CHR Training Tier 2. Bonus Action. On success target takes −2 to all rolls until end of their next turn AND must target you on their next action. Once per enemy per combat. Cannot be used on other player characters.
  • Rally: Unlocked at CHR Training Tier 6. Contested Intimidate vs Sense Motive against every enemy within 15ft. Those who fail take a scaling penalty to their next defense roll against you.
  • Creature Intimidation: Non-sapient creatures can be frightened off with Intimidate. DC scales with creature level. Failing by 5+ may provoke a charge.
  • Fylgja Intimidate: Gorilla, Boar, and Hyena-type Fylgja can make Intimidate checks independently.

Linguistics (CHR)

Language, code-breaking, written communication, and cultural fluency. Governs everything text and language-based that isn't direct conversation.

  • Read / Decode: Translating a foreign language, deciphering a coded document, or interpreting an ancient text. DC scales with obscurity and encryption complexity.
  • Forgery: Contested Linguistics vs an observer's Linguistics (or Awareness at DM discretion). On success your document passes.
  • Cultural Reading: In an unfamiliar region or faction environment, Linguistics helps you understand unspoken customs, signs, and social signals.
  • Magazine Reroll: A successful Linguistics check on an unfamiliar magazine lets you identify it and reroll before it auto-consumes — you understand what it does and can choose the right moment to trigger it.
  • Languages: Each language known expands communication range. CHR Training Tier 1 grants +2 Linguistics. New languages are learned at specific level milestones.

Nature (WIS)

Wilderness survival, creature knowledge, tracking, and substance identification. Mastery of the living world.

  • Creature Read: Bonus Action at combat start vs any non-human target. 12+ reveals one resistance or weakness. 22+ reveals all of them.
  • Tracking: In any non-urban environment, Nature vs target's Stealth (or DM-set DC). Success = reliable trail. Nat 20 = no further checks needed this session.
  • Identify Substance: Roll Nature against an unknown poison, plant, or compound. 12+ = general effect. 22+ = full ID; +3 to the next Heal check treating it.
  • Foraging: Nature check (12+) between sessions grants D6×5 Supplies. Nat 20 = D10×5 + one rare plant.
  • Nen Ecology Reading: In the presence of Nen-infused environments, high Nature rolls can reveal information about the local power ecology at DM discretion.

Nen (WIS)

The primary skill governing Nen ability use, Nen combat rolls, and aura-based interaction. Mutually exclusive with Natural Attunement — both use the soul as foundation.

  • Nen Ability Activation: All Nen abilities use the Nen skill or WIS modifier for attack rolls and contested checks. See Section 5 for the full Nen framework.
  • Aura Suppression: Suppress your aura as a Bonus Action to avoid Nen-sensing detection. While suppressed, Nen-based damage and skill checks take −2.
  • Nen Sensing: At WIS Training Tier 1+, Nen auras are sensed passively within normal detection radius regardless of Stealth.
  • Hatsu Expression: Unique Nen ability defined at WIS Training Tier 4. Scales with WIS investment. Higher rolls produce more powerful expressions.
  • Nen Disruption Resistance: WIS modifier is the primary defense stat when targeted by a Nen Disruption effect.

Perform (CHR)

Artistic expression, public presence, crowd management, and covenant recovery. Perform is the skill of projecting self to an audience — broader than entertainment.

  • Distraction: Bonus Action. Roll Perform vs nearest observer's Awareness. On success, one nearby ally making a Stealth check gets +3 this turn.
  • Morale: Bonus Action in combat. On 15+, one ally within 10ft gains +2 to their next roll. On 25+, that ally gains +4 instead. Single-target only — does not stack with Battle Cry in the same turn.
  • Social Cover: When maintaining a disguise or persona, Perform substitutes for Speech checks tied to the act of performance itself.
  • Pact Covenant Recovery: Characters with an active covenant can use a meaningful Perform action to partially recover Covenant Pool points. The performance must resonate with the covenant's character — a sun covenant responds to a sunrise ritual; a demonic pact responds accordingly. DC and recovery set by DM based on effort and resonance.
  • Animal Communication: At CHR Training Tier 7, Perform (alongside Speech) can communicate with any animal or non-hostile creature.

Ride (INT)

Mounted combat, vehicle operation, and high-speed maneuvering. Ride scales with Intelligence — reading vehicle mechanics and mount behavior is as much mental as physical.

  • Mounted Advantage: Passive — while fighting from a moving vehicle or mount, gain +1 to hit and +1 to defense. No check required.
  • Chase: Contested Ride rolls each round. Higher result closes or widens gap by 2 squares. Gap at 30 = escape. Gap at 0 = intercept or ram.
  • Ram: Use Ride roll as the attack roll. On hit, deal D6 + Ride modifier damage to target; vehicle takes half.
  • Vehicle Proficiency: At INT Training Tier 4, choose a vehicle type. Gain +3 Ride with that type permanently.
  • Evasive Driving: When a vehicle is targeted by an attack, Bonus Action Ride roll. On a result above the attacker's roll, the attack misses.

Sense Motive (WIS)

Reading people — detecting lies, gauging intent, and understanding what someone actually wants versus what they say. The primary defense against social manipulation.

  • Detect Deception: Contested vs the speaker's Speech, Perform, or Linguistics. On success you sense something is off; a high margin reveals specifically what.
  • Read Disposition: DC 12 reveals an NPC's current disposition tier. DC 20 reveals what would shift it.
  • Resist Intimidate: When targeted by combat Intimidate, Sense Motive is the defense roll. Higher result negates the effect.
  • Anticipate Action: In negotiations or standoffs, a Sense Motive check (DM-set DC) can reveal an NPC's next likely move — whether they're about to concede, escalate, or signal allies.
  • Surface Read: At WIS Training Tier 10, Sense Motive passively reads surface thoughts of anyone in sight (+10 Sense Motive bonus).

Speech (CHR)

Persuasion, negotiation, diplomacy, and direct interpersonal influence. The default skill for any attempt to change someone's mind or behavior through words.

  • Persuasion: Opposed Speech vs target's Sense Motive for resistant targets; DM-set DC for willing/neutral. Margin determines outcome per the Social Encounter table. See Section 14.
  • Negotiation: Extended social encounters use Speech as the primary check. Failures don't end attempts unless margin is bad enough to shift disposition.
  • Deception: Telling a convincing lie or maintaining a false identity in conversation. Contested vs target's Sense Motive.
  • Faction Interaction: Checkpoints, faction representatives, and bureaucracy — Speech determines how much cooperation and goodwill you extract.
  • City Contacts: Speech investment unlocks city contacts granting +3 Speech/Awareness in that city and advance threat warning. See Section 12.
  • Auto-20: At CHR Training Tier 3, once per session declare an automatic 20 on a Speech check before rolling.

Stealth (DEX)

Concealment, silent movement, and avoiding detection. Always an active state — going undetected requires investment and the environment works against you in contested zones.

  • Stealth Movement: Moving while sneaking costs double movement. A character with 6 base movement has 3 effective squares per turn while hidden.
  • Stay Hidden: Contested vs observer's Awareness. Beating by 1–4 = presence sensed, no location. Beating by 5+ = completely undetected.
  • Assassination Strike: Successful Stealth approach followed by an attack — margin requirements scale by tier. On meeting the margin, attack is an automatic critical. See Section 7 for the full table.
  • Aura Suppression: Nen users in stealth must suppress their aura as a Bonus Action (−2 to Nen damage and checks while suppressed) to hide from Nen-sensitive targets.
  • Stealth Refresh: Standard tier: every 3 turns | Elite: every 5 turns | Legendary/Immortal: every 7 turns.
  • Pickpocket: Stealth + Thieving vs target's Awareness. Base +5 difficulty; +7 if target is aware or item is large; +10 if both.
  • Fylgja Stealth: Fox, Jaguar, Panther, Cat, and Chameleon-type Fylgja are Stealth-capable and can operate hidden independently.

Technology (INT)

Electronic systems, digital infrastructure, augmentation, and anything that runs on code or circuitry. Engineering builds and fixes; Technology operates and exploits.

  • Hacking / System Access: Gaining unauthorized access to electronic systems, overriding security, or extracting data. DC scales with security level.
  • Gadget Operation: Activating and deploying crafted gadgets. At INT Training Tier 4, Energy Cell pool = INT modifier × 10.
  • Electronic Warfare: Disrupting communications, jamming signals, spoofing electronic identities. Stealth Tech specialization path substitutes Technology for Stealth near electronics.
  • Augment Interface: Characters with cybernetic augments use Technology to interface with, upgrade, or repair their own systems.
  • Vehicle Systems: Accessing or overriding vehicle electronics (comms, autopilot, weapons locks) uses Technology rather than Ride or Engineering.
  • Auto-Success: At INT Training Tier 4+, once per session declare an automatic success on any Engineering or Technology check before rolling.

Thieving (DEX)

Sleight of hand, lockpicking, pickpocketing, and manual subterfuge. Covers all fine-motor criminal applications that Stealth alone cannot resolve.

  • Pickpocket: Contested Thieving vs target's Awareness. Base +5 difficulty; +7 if target is aware or item is large/heavy; +10 if both.
  • Lockpicking: Roll Thieving against the lock's DC. Standard locks DC 10–14; security locks DC 16–20; military/faction locks DC 22+. Fail by 5+ triggers a trap or alarm if present.
  • Sleight of Hand: Planting an item on a target, palming an object, or swapping an item in plain sight. Contested Thieving vs Awareness.
  • Disarm Trap: Mechanical traps use Thieving; electronic traps use Technology. Failing by 5+ triggers the trap.
  • Fylgja Thieving: Snake, Spider, and Ferret-type Fylgja are Thieving-capable and can perform physical theft independently.

10. Weapon Grades — Damage, Elements, and Grade Problems

Damage ranges are base dice before modifiers. Modifiers (Strength, Dexterity, proficiency bonuses, Tech/Nen effects) stack on top. A Grade 3 weapon in the hands of a Level 20 character with Str +12 and weapon proficiency +5 still deals D8 base — the grade sets the floor; the character raises the ceiling.

Grade 1 — Basic Pocket knives, tools, improvised weapons, broken glass

Damage: D3 (1–3) Typical elements: Physical only. Cannot be meaningfully augmented. Afflictions possible: None. Grade 1 weapons are too weak to reliably force status conditions through any normal damage pathway. Environmental augments (a poisoned blade, a burning surface) add procs from their own source, not from the weapon's grade. Viable against: Level 1–4 characters, unarmored NPCs. Completely absorbed by most armor at Level 6+. The problem at higher levels: Grade 1 weapons against any character with Con Training tier 2+ (+25 HP) or armor will routinely deal minimum damage even on good rolls. Against Level 15+ targets, a Grade 1 hit is an annoyance. Against Legendary-tier targets, it's cosmetic — they may not even bother tracking it. These weapons are only dangerous in volume (a mob of Standard-tier enemies with knives) or in very specific circumstances (poison-coated, surprise, zero armor). Against vehicle armor, Grade 1 deals nothing.

Grade 2 — Skillful Combat knives, hunting weapons, light firearms, quality tools used as weapons

Damage: D4–D6 (1–6) Typical elements: Physical; can carry simple coatings (Poison, Fire if treated). A skilled user can maintain an elemental coat for 2 turns before it degrades. Afflictions possible: Poison (if coated), Fire proc possible if treated, but requires maintaining the coat through the fight. Viable against: Level 1–8 characters, lightly armored opponents. Against Level 12+ with moderate Con, Grade 2 struggles to penetrate meaningful armor without significant margin advantages. Combos worth noting: Grade 2 + Poison coating → Poison proc at normal rate. Grade 2 + Fire coat → Fire proc, but the coat burns out after 2 hits. In the right hands, Grade 2 weapons used against Soaked targets with Electric coating can still trigger Conductance — the grade doesn't limit the interaction, just the base damage ceiling. The problem at higher levels: Armor HP begins outpacing Grade 2 base damage at Level 10–12. A character with 20 Armor HP who takes a Grade 2 hit on an average roll takes nothing to their actual HP — the armor absorbs it entirely. Grade 2 weapons require exceptional margin bonuses (from Dex proficiency, high rolls) to matter past Level 14. Against Heavy or Super Heavy vehicles, Grade 2 deals nothing.

Grade 3 — Master Standard swords, combat knives, military firearms, most player-tier weapons at session start

Damage: D6–D12 (1–12 depending on weapon type) - Light: D6 (half swords, combat knives, sidearms) - Standard: D8 (katanas, pistols, standard swords) - Heavy: D10–D12 (two-handed swords, rifles, heavy pistols)

Typical elements: Physical baseline. Readily accepts augments (Int Training tier 4). Elemental coatings last the full combat if maintained. Most Nen-based augments work with Grade 3. Afflictions possible: Full access to all standard procs based on element applied. A Grade 3 sword with a Fire augment procs fire at normal rate. With Electric + Water setup, Grade 3 can trigger Conductance at doubled dice — the grade doesn't cap elemental interactions. Viable against: All levels, with diminishing standalone damage impact above Level 20. Still mechanically relevant at high levels because elemental interactions and margin bonuses scale independently of grade. Combos worth noting: - Grade 3 + Ice augment vs a Soaked target → Water+Ice = Flash Freeze Puddle without needing a separate hit - Grade 3 Electric + prior Water damage → Conductance (doubled Electric base) - Grade 3 + Poison on a target already Radiating → Compounding Toxicity (Radiation can't be cleared while Poisoned) The problem at higher levels: D6–D12 base damage against Level 25+ characters with 200+ HP is 1–6% per hit before armor. Grade 3 weapons at Legendary/Immortal tier become tools for status effects and elemental setup rather than raw damage instruments. Characters using Grade 3 at high levels should be building toward interactions, not hoping to win through base damage. Against Heavy/Super Heavy vehicles: half damage to armor.

Grade 4 — Supreme Lightsabers, modified high-output weapons, Arc Staffs, precision-engineered firearms, military-grade explosives

Damage: D10–D20 or multi-dice (1–20+ per hit) - Light: D10–D12 (precision sidearms, single lightsaber blade) - Standard: D6+D8 or 2D8 (two-handed variants, specialized martial weapons) - Heavy/Multi: 2D8–2D10 (Arc Staffs, dual-wield systems, heavy platform weapons) - Explosive-type: D20 single target or D12 AoE

Typical elements: Most Grade 4 weapons have an intrinsic element built into their design — lightsabers are Nen or Fire, the Arc Staff is Electric, the Alien Blaster is Electric, the Power Fist is Physical-force. This element applies passively on every hit without needing augments or coatings. Additional elements can stack through augments. Afflictions possible: Full proc access plus grade-specific effects. Grade 4 weapons have an improved proc threshold — the tier baseline is treated as one higher (Standard-tier Grade 4 attackers proc on ≤5 instead of ≤4; Elite on ≤6, etc.). This reflects the weapon's refinement carrying more force through the hit. Vehicle armor: Standard Grade 4 weapons deal half damage to Heavy/Super Heavy vehicle armor as normal. Specific Grade 4 weapons (DM-designated intrinsic-element or exotic-material weapons) penetrate fully. Viable against: All levels. Threatening at Level 9, serious at Level 15, still relevant at Level 30 due to multi-die scaling with modifiers. Combos worth noting: - Arc Staff (Electric) + prior Water damage → Conductance doubled, already built in - Lightsaber (Nen/Fire) against a Shadow-Blackouted target → Shadow + Nen reaction = Extended Blackout - Dual Grade 4 weapons → can carry two different elemental types, setting up interactions in a single turn The problem at higher levels: Even Grade 4 will start feeling the HP ceiling at Level 25+ against Legendary-tier enemies (300–500 HP). The grade's advantage at that tier is the intrinsic element and the lower proc threshold, not raw base damage. Grade 4 weapons are the workhorse of the entire Level 10–25 range.

Grade 5 — Exalted Weapons that have started wars. Forged once per era.

Damage: 2D20–4D20 or equivalent (base 2–80 per hit), often with additional fixed bonuses Intrinsic elements: Always present, often multiple. A Grade 5 weapon might deal Physical + Fire + Light simultaneously, applying three proc tables at once. The element cannot be removed or suppressed by normal means. Afflictions possible: Grade 5 weapons access the legendary negative tier of afflictions — effects that ordinarily require specific conditions or Nen Exorcism to create. Each Grade 5 weapon has one or two unique legendary afflictions defined at the time of creation that no other weapon can consistently produce: - Example: A sword that severs the target's connection to Nen for the rest of combat on a critical hit - Example: A weapon that applies a permanent Attribute penalty (−1 to a random Attribute) on any hit that reduces the target below half HP - Example: A weapon whose wounds cannot be healed by anything short of a legendary-tier Heal roll while the wielder is still alive Vehicle armor: Always penetrates. All vehicle classes. All armor values. Full damage. Viable against: Everyone. At Immortal tier, Grade 5 weapons remain dangerous because of the legendary afflictions, the multi-die scaling, and the intrinsic multi-element procs — not just base damage numbers. The problem at lower levels: Grade 5 weapons often have wielder requirements — a character below a certain attribute threshold, training tier, or narrative condition may be unable to use them at all, or may use them at reduced effectiveness. An Exalted weapon in untrained hands is a liability as much as an asset.

Grade 6 Weapons of this classification are not understood through conventional frameworks. What is known: they exist, they are unique, and they are not obtainable through any normal means.

Damage: Beyond measurement Vehicle armor: Penetrates all classes without exception Viable against: Everyone at every tier without exception Creation: Unknown. How these weapons come to exist is not publicly understood and is among the most closely guarded knowledge in the world. The problem: These weapons are finite. Each use burns the soul within. When the soul is consumed, the weapon typically degrades. They are also aware — using one is a negotiation, not a mechanical transaction. How many exist, where they are, and who holds them is largely unknown.

Grade 6 Armor How it is created and how it functions are not publicly understood. It exists. Beyond that, it is rumor.

Grade 6 armor absorbs all incoming damage completely. No damage reaches the wearer. It cannot be repaired. Every hit absorbed is permanent durability loss — when it fails, it fails completely.

It cannot be combined with a Grade 6 weapon.

Magazines — Skill Consumables

Magazines are passive, skill-specific consumables found scattered throughout the world — in ruins, faction caches, black market stalls, libraries, and the personal effects of fallen enemies. Each magazine corresponds to exactly one skill. When a character holds a magazine and rolls a check using that skill, the magazine is automatically consumed to grant a flat +5 bonus to that roll. No action is required to use it.

Magazines do not stack in effect — only one is consumed per roll regardless of how many of that type a character carries. A character can hold multiple magazines for the same skill and will burn through them one at a time as that skill comes up.

Recognition: The first time a character finds a magazine they do not know what it does until reading it (Awareness or Linguistics check, or downtime). After that, they recognize that type on sight.

Acquisition: Magazines are world-placed only — not purchasable through standard Supplies or faction prestige. They reward exploration and going off the critical path.

Tracking: Each character sheet includes a magazine counter column (MAG) in the Skills section to the left of the skill modifier. The counter tracks how many magazines of each type the character currently holds.

13. Karma System

Karma is a passive measure of a character's moral standing as judged by the world — not by any faction, not by any ideology, but by the cumulative weight of their actions over time. It is tracked by the DM and reflected to players only as a title. Players never see an exact number.

How It Works

Every character begins at 0 Karma. The range runs from −1000 to +1000. The DM assigns karma gains or losses in the moment based on the nature of an action — there is no fixed chart. Saving a village and assassinating a political rival are not the same weight, and the DM judges each accordingly.

Karma does not punish playstyle — it reflects it. A character who operates in moral grey areas will hover near Neutral. A character who consistently protects the innocent will climb. A character who leaves a trail of bodies and broken communities will fall.

Karma title is not a game-mechanical modifier — it is a roleplay signal. NPCs who know your reputation respond accordingly. A Saint walks into a desperate community and people reach toward them. 14. Social & Roleplay Systems

These rules govern the non-combat half of the campaign — encounters built around people, information, tension, and consequence rather than initiative and damage rolls. They use the same skill system as combat but with different stakes and a different resolution structure.

Social Encounter Framework

Social interactions fall into three categories based on whether the target has reason to resist.

Unopposed (willing or neutral target): Roll the appropriate social skill against a DC set by the DM based on context. Standard difficulty class table applies — Easy (8), Moderate (12), Hard (16), Very Hard (20).

Opposed (resistant target): Contested roll. The character rolls Speech, Intimidate, or Perform. The target rolls Sense Motive. Higher result wins.

Extended (major social goal spanning multiple exchanges): DM sets a number of required successes. Each meaningful interaction is one check. Failures don't end the attempt unless the margin is bad enough to shift disposition.

Graduated outcomes — margin from target DC or opposing roll:

Result Outcome
Fail by 5+ Refused AND disposition drops one tier
Fail by 1–4 Refused; no relationship change
Beat by 1–4 Partial compliance or compliance with conditions
Beat by 5–9 Full compliance
Beat by 10+ Full compliance; target volunteers additional help, information, or goodwill

Social successes open doors — they do not override a target's core motivations, faction loyalties, or self-preservation instincts.

NPC Disposition

Every significant NPC has a disposition tier that affects how social interactions with them resolve. The DM tracks this. Players can attempt to read it with a Sense Motive check — DC 12 reveals the current tier, DC 20 reveals what would shift it.

Disposition Social Check Modifier Behavior
Hostile −5 May attack or obstruct; will not engage in good faith
Unfriendly −2 Short answers, reluctant, no favors
Neutral ±0 Standard interaction, no assumptions
Friendly +2 Cooperative, willing to help within reason
Allied +4 Proactively assists; shares information freely; goes out of their way

Shifting upward requires either a strong social success (beat by 10+) or a meaningful narrative action that the NPC genuinely values — not just a check result.

Shifting downward happens from a bad failure (fail by 5+), a discovered betrayal, a faction conflict, or a public action the NPC finds objectionable.

Disposition does not reset between sessions. It reflects the accumulated history between this character and this NPC.

Notoriety & Heat

Heat does not apply before Level 6. Below that threshold, characters have not yet built the profile, resources, or incident history to register meaningfully on faction intelligence networks. At Level 6, World Notoriety becomes a factor — actions begin to carry a public record, and factions start paying attention.

From Level 6 onward, Heat reflects how much of the world is actively aware of and responding to the character.

Heat Tier Effect
Unknown No recognition. Standard interactions. All characters below Level 6 remain here regardless of actions.
Recognized Occasional recognition in informed circles. +5 DC on all Stealth checks in populated areas. Faction agents may report confirmed sightings.
Wanted Active bounty publicly known. Dedicated trackers possible. −3 to all social checks with unfamiliar NPCs. Checkpoint stops automatic in hostile territory.
Infamous Active multi-faction pursuit. Most informed NPCs react with fear or caution. Some allies distance themselves under external pressure. Cannot reduce below Wanted through normal means — only major narrative events shift this tier.

Heat increases from: combat in populated areas, failed public social checks, positive faction sightings, high-profile actions, large bounty postings.

Heat decreases from: extended quiet periods, successful bribery of the right parties, deliberate reputation management, identity concealment. Dropping from Wanted to Recognized requires sustained effort over multiple sessions. Dropping from Infamous requires a narrative-level event.

Heat tier maps directly to the bounty tiers in Part 1. Notable bounty = Recognized. High Value = Wanted. Extreme or Most Wanted = Infamous.

Scene Pressure Mechanics

Roleplay scenes carry consequence through pressure — forces that change the situation if not addressed. The tension is real even without a combat encounter.

Pressure Clock: The DM tracks a hidden beat count before the scene changes state. Beats pass as time is spent, checks are made, and significant things happen. Players feel pressure through NPC behavior, environmental cues, and pacing — not through an announced number.

Escalating Scene Tension: Failed checks in social or infiltration scenes accumulate tension.

Scene Tension Effect
0–2 No change
3 NPCs become visibly suspicious; passive checks against the character increase by +3
5 Active investigation begins; movement through the area becomes restricted
7 Confrontation — the scene forces a resolution, peaceful or otherwise

Succeeding on a check reduces scene tension by 1. A strong success (beat by 10+) reduces it by 2. Tension resets fully when the scene ends.

Hard Deadlines: Some events are time-locked regardless of player action. The DM announces these at the scene's start — the player knows the window exists and makes decisions accordingly. Hard deadlines create real urgency without removing agency.

Faction Patrols and Checkpoints: In controlled or surveilled environments, failing a Stealth or social check against a patrol adds 2 to scene tension rather than immediately triggering conflict. This gives the character one beat to recover before the situation escalates further.

Bribery & Negotiation

Bribery is a Speech check with money attached. The DC and base cost scale with the risk the target is taking, not the character's preference.

Ask DC Base Cost
Minor favor — look away, share basic info 10–14 Low — thousands
Meaningful favor — delay an action, provide access 15–19 Moderate — hundreds of thousands
Major favor — destroy records, provide a name 20–25 High — millions
Extreme ask — betray faction, commit a crime 26+ Extraordinary — may not be achievable at any price

On success: Favor completed as agreed. On failure by 1–4: Refused. Target stays quiet about the attempt. On failure by 5+: Refused and reported. Scene tension increases by 3 and disposition drops one tier.

Charisma Training tier 7's price-halving applies to all bribery base costs permanently. A failed bribe at the extreme tier is a major incident regardless of margin — some targets will act on it immediately.

Contacts System

A contact is a named NPC with an established relationship (Friendly or Allied disposition) and a defined specialty — what they can uniquely provide.

Using a contact: Once per session, a character may call on a contact for their specialty. The DC scales with the personal risk to the contact.

Risk to Contact DC to Assist
No personal risk 8
Minor risk — professional or social 14
Moderate risk — legal or physical 20
High risk — life or faction standing 26

Disposition modifier applies: Friendly = +2, Allied = +4. On failure, the contact cannot help this session but the relationship is unaffected — unless the failure margin is 10+, which drops disposition one tier.

Contact specialties are defined by what the NPC does in the world. An information broker provides intelligence. A black market dealer provides access. A faction officer provides clearance or protection. A contact cannot provide something outside their realistic reach — asking a street-level fixer for a military extraction is not within their specialty regardless of their disposition.

Contacts are tracked on the character sheet under Notable Relationships. Using a contact for something dangerous may require reciprocation. Favors have weight in this world.

Downtime Activities

Between sessions, the character has time to act on the world outside of active encounters. Each downtime period allows one primary activity and one minor activity. Minor activities include a single shopping trip, a brief conversation, or a short journey — anything that doesn't require a full commitment of time.

Primary Activities — choose one per downtime period:

Rest Fully: Restore all HP, Armor HP, and status effects to baseline. All resources reset. No other primary activity may be taken. This is the only way to clear persistent status effects outside of field treatment.

Train: Dedicate time toward the next attribute training tier. Requires a teacher with a higher training level than your own. Counts as meaningful progress — the DM determines how many downtime sessions of training constitute a full tier unlock based on the tier's difficulty.

Gather Intel: Make an Intelligence or Awareness check (DC 14) to learn one specific piece of confirmed information about an upcoming location, NPC, or faction. On success, the DM provides one accurate answer. On failure, nothing is confirmed — rumors may still surface but their accuracy is not guaranteed.

Strengthen a Contact: Make a Speech or Perform check (DC = 10 + number of tiers already shifted upward for this contact). On success, shift one contact's disposition up one tier. Cannot shift a contact above Allied.

Acquire Resources: Conduct one shopping trip or black market visit. Standard pricing applies unless the character has Charisma Training tier 7 (prices permanently halved) or specific faction standing that affects access.

Lie Low: Reduce Heat by one tier. Requires the full downtime period — no other primary activity. Cannot reduce below Unknown. Dropping from Infamous requires a narrative-level event even with a Lie Low action.
